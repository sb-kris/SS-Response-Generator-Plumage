How Flock inherits the brand

  • Positioning: lead with the external phrase — "customer intelligence and feedback orchestration platform." Flock is internal tooling, but every line is graduation-ready, so we hold to that bar.
  • Colour: SurveySparrow teal #4A9CA6 appears in Flock only where SurveySparrow itself is being referenced — connection-status pills, the deck's "SurveySparrow shows the show" card, push-complete moments.
  • Logo: use the parent sparrow symbol exactly as supplied. Never recolour, restroke, or compose it inside a frame. It carries the parent's authority unchanged.
  • Voice: inherit the warmth — conversational and direct — but skip the marketing flourish. Flock speaks to practitioners, not prospects.
  • Trust signals: when Flock is on a screen that customer data could touch, the parent's compliance badges (SOC, ISO 27001, HIPAA, GDPR, CCPA, CSA STAR) apply. Don't claim them on Flock's behalf; do respect them.

On SurveySparrow's typography

SurveySparrow's public brand leans on a friendly humanist sans-serif — warm but not playful. Flock doesn't try to match it pixel-for-pixel. Geist sits in the same family of clean, neutral sans-serifs, so the two brands visually agree without one being a copy of the other. When embedding SurveySparrow content (screenshots, mocks, the sparrow mark) inside Flock surfaces, keep their typography untouched — never re-type SurveySparrow's wordmark in Geist.

Voice & tone

Measured. Specific. Plainspoken.

Flock is internal tooling for SEs, Implementation, and CSMs — busy practitioners. Skip marketing flourish; respect their time with words that earn their place on the screen.

✓ Do
  • Lead with the trade-off: "so the hours go back to customer problems."
  • Use concrete numbers where they exist (response count, $/response, latency).
  • Short sentences. Then short sentences. Then one that runs a touch longer to make the point land.
  • Acknowledge tooling has limits — "you can walk away mid-run."
  • Name the specific feature when claiming AI work: Echo, Wing AI, CogniVue, SmartReach, Co-Pilot, Enrich.
✗ Don't
  • "Unfair advantage" · "kill the room" · "10x" — hype words break the trust contract.
  • "Empower" · "supercharge" · "delight" — meaningless verbs.
  • "Best-in-class" · "revolutionary" · "next-generation" — explain what it does instead.
  • "AI-powered everything" — name the actual feature doing the work.
  • Over-promise. If the LLM is rate-limited, say so.
  • Use exclamation marks. Confidence speaks softly.
